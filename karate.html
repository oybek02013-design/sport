Karate (Japanese: 空手, Hepburn: KARATE; lit. 'empty hand') is a Japanese martial art, combat sport, and Olympic sport (since 1964). It was developed in the early 20th century in Okinawa, Japan, and later spread to mainland Japan and the rest of the world. Karate emphasizes striking techniques such as punches, kicks, knee strikes, and elbow strikes. The objective of competitive karate is to score points by landing techniques on an opponent's torso or head. Karate's philosophy revolves around the principles of discipline, respect, and self-improvement. Practitioners train in a structured environment that emphasizes mental and physical development. The art has evolved into various styles, each with its own unique characteristics and training methods. The international governing body for karate is the World Karate Federation (WKF), and competitors participate in the international WKF professional circuit.
